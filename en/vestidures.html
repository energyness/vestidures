Emancipating the fast fashion industry
to achieve conscious consumption
and liberated apparel

Over the last decades, capitalist society has brought the fashion industry to adopt an accelerated rhythm of production, connected with the FAST concept. This has unleashed a rampant form of consumerism, which does not satisfy our fashion needs, since it encourages us this to live dress and consume in an alienated and impersonal way. We totally reject this lifestyle based on a consumerism by and for itself (and not aimed at satisfying specific needs). A lifestyle that buries us with stuff and empties our inner selves.

We like clothes and we enjoy getting dressed, that's why we think it is necessary to find alternative ways to update our clothing. We are interested in second hand markets, cloth swaps and small firms that produce locally. These alternatives are a starting point for experimentation.
